10. Let's think about the number of multiplications performed per pixel of F. Assuming F is much larger than a 3x3 filter:
    * $F * (G * G^T)$ This performs some constant and negligible number of multiplications to compute $G * G^T$; the exact number depends on how you think of padding, but it should be small compared to F's size. Once that'd one, we compute 9 multiplications per pixel of $F$ to convolve the 3x3 $G*G^T$ filter by all of F.
    * $(F * G) * G^T$ Performs 3 multiplications per pixel to compute $(F * G)$, then 3 more per pixel to convolve the result with $G^T$, for a total of 6 multiplications per pixel. This will be more efficient, with less than 2/3 the number of multiplications.
