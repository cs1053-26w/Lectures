# Lecture 6 Problems - Solutions

For each transformation pictured below, find a 2x2 transformation matrix that maps the original (left) image's coordinates to the corresponding point in the right image. In all cases, the right image's (0, 0) point is at the bottom left corner of the image (bottom corner of the image, in the case of #3.) Also in all images, $x$ points right and $y$ points up.

1. 
    $$
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1.3 & 0 \\
    0   & 1.3
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$

2. 
    $$
    \begin{bmatrix}
    2 & 0 \\
    0 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$

3. 
    $$
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0.25 \\
    0   & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$

4. 
    Let $a = 30 * \pi / 180 \approx 0.524$  , i.e., 30 degrees in radians.
    $$
    \begin{bmatrix}
     \cos a & -\sin a \\
     \sin a & \cos a
    \end{bmatrix}
    = \begin{bmatrix}
     \sqrt{3}/2 & -1/2 \\
    1/2  &  \sqrt{3}/2
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$

5. 
    $$
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & 40\\
    0 & 1 & 40\\
    0 & 0 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$
